ex-professional troll

“Caster, Commentator, and overall Goof” is a simple description of I am. I work full time as an IT analyst, and I’m looking to change that by shifting my focus to a career in production and esports. I don’t call myself a “streamer,” instead my Twitch channel has always served as a laboratory for my experiments in production and design. Many of my shows are answers to a simple question: “Can I do it with what I have?”

My latest projects are focused on what I can do for the esports industry. Production and commentary projects are a showcase of my passion for sharing a fun game with a wider audience. My dream would be to create a show that would make esports accessible for all, including loved ones and strangers. We’re all here to have a good time.

At the Chicago Gaming Coalition in March 2018, I was asked to enter, then run, then commentate a Windjammers tournament. I fell in love with the game and joined the Windjammers community. I contributed by streaming their reoccuring online Fightcade tournaments to my Twitch channel.

I am currently by hosting “Windjammers Wednesday” on my Twitch channel. I have played and worked at multiple Windjammers tournaments, including Frosty Faustings, Combo Breaker, Dreamhack Dallas, and EVO.

Gamer’s Headquarters (or GHQ for short) is an internet cafe/gaming center that ran a weekly Hearthstone tournament. In February 2019. they wanted to find a way to increase their attendance, and I offered to build a tournament stream. Without meaning to, I became engrossed with the production values of this project, scoping out equipment to bring the overall budget to under a grand. Players began to tell their friends about the tournaments because the top 8 players would be on camera. The number of attendees doubled within weeks.

This particular project made me realize how much I enjoyed producing. I found that I had a knack for building and running the whole show - I managed the scoreboard, camera angles, and voice talent. I improved the stream in pieces, starting with simple player camera shots, then adding commentators and brackets to fill out the overall audience experience. This project directly inspired me to pursue a production career in esports.

Personal Stream

Broadcast

When I was in college, I knew about Twitch, but never thought much about it. Then, while playing with a group of friends during September 2014, I was introduced to a “Twitch Streamer” in our voice call. After checking out his profile (Thanks FleeRancer!), I wondered if I could go live with my own channel. So I tried it; I did not expect to thoroughly enjoy streaming, but here we are.

I still worked full time, but I would come home and go live on Twitch with the intention to just tinker and experiment. I would play different games to test my hardware, or to gauge the audience reaction, or to see if I could split my focus between the game and the chat. While I watched other streamers perform full time and monetize their content, I simply continued to develop my show, and practice good streaming habits. I always focused on my positive persona, my musical background, and my audience interactions.

Currently I stream Windjammers Wednesdays, and occasionally a Windjammers Fightcade tournament.

DJ

Branding

Blame my dad for my DJ passion - he was mixing tunes for nightclubs before I was born. I had to sneak on his computer and make my first mix behind his back in 2002 before he would start to show me the ropes. High school nights were spent making hourlong mix CD’s for friends and family, but I didn’t perform on stage until college. I would be lying if I said I wasn’t there for the DJ courses.

At Columbia College Chicago, I was in the heart of the city learning from working house and techno artists. They taught me how to perform on analog equipment, but also how to promote myself with the latest social media platforms, how to get gigs, and how to brand myself for recognition. I played for a few years, ending up in a contest that flew me out to Los Angeles.

  • Los Angeles Hype Video

After I finished, I knew that I wanted to produce my own music and shows. The idea of being just another DJ cramped my sense of creativity. I put DJing aside to focus on my other projects, and will revisit that performance art when I’m ready.

Corporate Experience

IT Analysis

I was in middle school when I took instructional lessons and an apprenticeship at Shodor. All through high school, I was experimenting with Linux on my own laptop while developing online applications after school. I went to Columbia College Chicago for a bachelors in video game programming, but it turned out I wasn't cut out to play with code for a living.

After college, I have been working in IT Analyst roles full time, focusing on root-cause troubleshooting and team communication. I want common issues to be resolved and abolished so that I could continue to work on my professional development. I picked up CompTIA A+ and Network+ certifications along the way.

I become involved with a project after work by helping produce audio recordings of poet recitals. The Teen Writers and Artists Program (or TWAAP for short) is a group focused on exposing high school students to writing and performing spoken word poetry. They work closely with public schools in Illinois to bring more exposure to the world's largest youth poetry slam, Louder Than A Bomb.

TWAAP coaxed me into writing, then performing spoken word poetry as well. For years I had always doubted my writing abilities, and these amazing folks simply accepted me as one of them. Occasionally I still put thoughts to paper in poetry form, if only to keep up with everything going on.

Stilts

Fitness

I have wanted jumping stilts ever since sophomore year of high school. My background in various basic circus arts (thank you Waldorf education) had prepared me to easily learn how to run in jumping stilts in a single demonstration session.

I had been itching to use jumping stilts again, and the opportunity presented itself. My IT job had a benefit to reimburse fitness expenses. Since I was not interested in attending a gym regularly, I fulfilled my heart’s desire and acquired my stilts.

First goal - running in the silly things, then playing Frisbee with them!
