“Caster, Commentator, and overall Goof” is a simple description of I am. I work full time as an IT analyst, and I’m looking to change that by shifting my focus to a career in production and esports. I don’t call myself a “streamer,” instead my Twitch channel has always served as a laboratory for my experiments in production and design. Many of my shows are answers to a simple question: “Can I do it with what I have?”

My latest projects are focused on what I can do for the esports industry. Production and commentary projects are a showcase of my passion for sharing a fun game with a wider audience. My dream would be to create a show that would make esports accessible for all, including loved ones and strangers. We’re all here to have a good time.

Designed by Kittanie.com

At the Chicago Gaming Coalition, I was asked to enter, then run, then commentate a free Windjammers (WJ) tournament. While the stream didn’t happen that day, I joined the WJ community and became a regular streamer for their online Fightcade tournaments.

I began attending WJ tournaments (Frosty Faustings, Combo Breaker, Dreamhack Dallas, EVO) as a player, as a TO, and as a commentator.

When MassVR moved to their latest location, I became a regular member. I was excited for their innovative use for VR equipment, and quickly recognized the value in their growing virtual esport. I discussed the value of having a stream on Twitch to showcase their “VR Champions” team battle game, and CEO Chris Lai allowed me to build the production prototype. I built the Twitch channel, and went live as the main commentator, setting the production standard for future use and allowing players a chance to replay their games for their friends all over the world.

Stilts

Fitness

I have wanted jumping stilts ever since sophomore year of high school. My background in various basic circus arts (thank you Waldorf education) had prepared me to easily learn how to run in jumping stilts in a single demonstration session.

I had been itching to use jumping stilts again, and the opportunity presented itself. My IT job had a benefit to reimburse fitness expenses. Since I was not interested in attending a gym regularly, I fulfilled my heart’s desire and acquired my stilts.

First goal - running in the silly things, then playing Frisbee with them!

Gamer’s Headquaters, or GHQ for short, is an internet cafe/gaming center with a focus on the Smash Brothers community. The staff would run a small Hearthstone tournament weekly, drawing the same dozen or so players. I implemented a plan to have the tournaments streamed on Twitch, while on a tight budget of less than a thousand dollars.

After launching the stream, the tournament numbers swelled to more than double the attendees than normal. I would manage the onstream commentators and the bracket, while adding a joke or two of my own over the mic. This project was truly fun for me, and inspired me to pursue a production career in esports.

  • Date: February 2019
  • GHQTV: Youtube

Personal Stream

Broadcast

While in a game with a few friends, I vividly remember being told to keep the conversation clean because a new party member was actively streaming. After checking out the whole Twitch phenomenon, I was curious to see if I could start my own. I opened Pandora’s Box and began to produce content on t.tv/jeffvaderjr.

The focus of my on-and-off streaming was on experimentation. I played whatever game suited my fancy simply to see how well it would work on my hardware, or how well it would respond to new viewers, or simply to see if I could hold a conversation with chat with good spirits. I played a multitude of games (LoL, CSGO, PUBG, various Indie and party titles, etc), tried out new camera setups, and even DJ’ed a few times.

While I never knew what I was going to do when I went live, I always focused on my positive persona, my musical background, and my audience interactions every show. Currently I stream Windjammers Wednesdays, and occasionally a WJ Fightcade tournament.

  • Date: September 2014
  • JeffVaderjr: Twitch Vod's

DJ

Branding

My father was a nightclub DJ before I was born. I picked up the basics by using my dad’s MixMeister software and making hour long mixes for my family and friends. In college, I was instructed by Chicago’s finest house DJs to perform live on analog equipment and to market myself accordingly. For a few years I played around town, even won a contest that flew me out to LA.

  • CAMPUS DJ: Pilot Season Championship Video

After I finished my set, I knew that I wanted to produce music and shows, rather than be held to the format of a standard nightclub DJ. I put DJing aside to focus on my other projects, and will revisit that performance art when I can.

  • Date: September 2002

Corporate Experience

IT Analysis

I was in middle school when I took instructional lessons and an apprenticeship at Shodor. All through high school, I was experimenting with Linux on my own laptop while developing online applications after school. I went to Columbia College Chicago for a bachelors in video game programming, but it turned out I wasn't cut out to play with code for a living.

After college, I have been working in IT Analyst roles full time, focusing on root-cause troubleshooting and team communication. I want common issues to be resolved and abolished so that I could continue to work on my professional development. I picked up CompTIA A+ and Network+ certifications along the way.

I become involved with a project after work by helping produce audio recordings of poet recitals. The Teen Writers and Artists Program (or TWAAP for short) is a group focused on exposing high school students to writing and performing spoken word poetry. They work closely with public schools in Illinois to bring more exposure to the world's largest youth poetry slam, Louder Than A Bomb.

TWAAP coaxed me into writing, then performing spoken word poetry as well. For years I had always doubted my writing abilities, and these amazing folks simply accepted me as one of them. Occasionally I still put thoughts to paper in poetry form, if only to keep up with everything going on.
